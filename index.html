About

iPoLS France is the French node of the international Physics of Living Systems (iPoLS) network, established as an International Research Network (IRN) by CNRS Biologie for the period 2026–2030. The network brings together researchers working at the interface of physics and the life sciences, united by a commitment to quantitative, multi-scale approaches that span from the molecular and cellular to the community level.

The French node federates more than fifteen laboratories and research federations spread across France — in Montpellier, Paris, Marseille, Grenoble, and Strasbourg — and is embedded in a wider international ecosystem that includes nodes in Italy, the United States, Germany, Israel, and beyond.

Our mission is to catalyze scientific collaborations, support early-career researchers through mobility grants and access to international events, and build a structured and visible national community at the physics–biology interface. The network is coordinated by Luca Ciandrini (Centre de Biologie Structurale, Université de Montpellier).
